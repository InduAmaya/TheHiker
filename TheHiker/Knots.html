Knots

Knowing how to tie some basic knots is an integral part of your backcountry skill set. Equally important is being able to recognize which knot is appropriate for which task.

Square Knot (Reef Knot)

This classic knot is useful in situations where you're connecting two pieces of rope. You can always add more pieces of rope, and this knot can be used almost anywhere, firstaid, especially when you need to tie something loosely to your pack, or make extra line for a tarp or rain fly. also it is easy to untie.

Sheet Bend

This knot is useful for connecting two pieces of rope of different sizes, in case you need a longer rope but are short on usable cordage.

Bowline

A bowline knot is the ticket when you need a loop that won't move. Backpackers can use it to tie off a bear bag or secure a canoe to shore. It is also classified as a rescue knot - you can tie it with one hand in cases where your other hand is immobilized.

Clove Hitch

Use to start and finish a lashing such as the conventional square lashing in pioneering. Also use for tying bandages (arm slings) in case of injuries and for first aid and suitable for tying off to tent pegs.
